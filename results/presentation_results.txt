================================================================================
CORE RESEARCH QUESTION
================================================================================
Does LLM refusal behavior decompose into domain-specific directions in hidden
state space, and can we surgically reduce over-refusal without compromising
general safety?


================================================================================
PART 1: MIDTERM RESULTS (Foundation)
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 1: Baseline Refusal Rates (after dataset cleaning)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Domain      Prompts  Refused  Answered  Refusal Rate
Harmful       100       98        2         98%
Medical       100       48       52         48%
Legal         100       63       37         63%

Note: Legal prompts were refined from ambiguous "edge case" prompts to
clearly illegal requests (counterfeiting, fraud, bribery, laundering).
This improved legal refusal rate from ~56% to 63%.

Key insight: Medical and legal domains show significant OVER-REFUSAL baseline
even before any ablation — the model refuses nearly half of legitimate
medical questions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 2: Mean-Difference Direction Extraction
------------------------------------------------------------------------
For each domain, we extract a direction vector:
  v_d = normalize( mean(H_refused) - mean(H_answered) )
  where H = hidden states at layer 16 across all prompts

Extracted vectors: v_harmful, v_medical, v_legal (each 4096-dimensional)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 3: Cosine Similarity Between Direction Vectors (Single Vectors)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Pair                    Cosine Similarity
harmful ↔ medical          -0.037  (near zero)
harmful ↔ legal             0.099  (near zero)
medical ↔ legal             0.112  (near zero)

Key insight: All pairwise cosine similarities are near zero → the three
refusal directions are GEOMETRICALLY INDEPENDENT. Ablating one domain's
refusal direction should not affect other domains.

Plot: heatmap.png

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 4: Single Vector Ablation — Initial Results
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ablation: x' = x - α*(x·v_d)*v_d  (projects out refusal direction)

v_harmful ablation on harmful prompts:
  α=0.0: 98% refusal (baseline)
  α=0.5: 98% refusal (no change)
  α=1.0:  0% refusal  ← complete elimination ✓

v_medical ablation on medical prompts:
  α=0.0: 48% refusal (baseline)
  α=0.5: 56% refusal (INCREASED — wrong direction!)
  α=1.0: 60% refusal (INCREASED further!)

v_legal ablation on legal prompts:
  α=0.0: 63% refusal (baseline, after dataset fix)
  α=0.5: 52% refusal (mild decrease)
  α=1.0: 50% refusal (mild decrease)

Key insight: Single vector ablation WORKS for harmful (clean direction)
but FAILS for medical (refusal INCREASES — concept erasure) and gives
only marginal improvement for legal.

Plot: refusal_rates_by_domain.png

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 5: SVD Analysis — Why Single Vector Fails (Effective Rank)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
We compute SVD of the difference matrix: D = H_refused - mean(H_answered)
Effective rank = exp(entropy of singular value distribution)
  → Measures how many dimensions encode refusal behavior

Domain    Effective Rank   Top-3 Singular Vectors Explain
Harmful        1.12         98.6%  0.5%  0.1%   ← nearly 1-dimensional!
Medical       31.35         15.0%  7.2%  6.7%   ← spread across 31+ dims
Legal         26.14         23.5% 10.2%  8.1%   ← spread across 26+ dims

Key insight: Harmful refusal is LOW-RANK (eff_rank ≈ 1) — a single
direction captures 98.6% of variance. Medical/legal refusal is
HIGH-RANK (eff_rank ≈ 26-31) — the refusal direction is entangled with
domain knowledge across many dimensions. This MATHEMATICALLY EXPLAINS
why single vector ablation causes concept erasure in medical: you can't
separate refusal from domain knowledge with one vector.

Plot: scree_plots.png

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULT 6: Principal Angles Between Domain Subspaces (Grassmann Geometry)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
We extract top-8 singular vectors per domain and compute principal angles
between each pair of subspaces via SVD of the cross-Gram matrix.

Subspace Pair         Principal Angles (degrees, top-5)        Mean
harmful ↔ medical     33.8°  54.7°  64.0°  75.8°  83.9°       72.0°
harmful ↔ legal       22.8°  48.3°  58.3°  69.2°  79.9°       67.9°
medical ↔ legal       25.6°  38.5°  45.1°  67.7°  73.0°       63.0°

Key insight: Angles range from ~23° to 84° (not all 90°, but far from 0°).
The subspaces are substantially independent — no pair shares a dominant
common direction. This extends the single-vector cosine similarity result
(which only checked top directions) to the full multi-dimensional subspace.

Plot: principal_angles.png

RESULT 8: The Structural Contrast — Why Harmful Is Different
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Extending the alpha sweep to the HARMFUL domain reveals a critical asymmetry.
Both single vector and subspace ablation were also run on harmful prompts.

HARMFUL DOMAIN — Single Vector Ablation:
  α=0.0:  98% refusal, PPL=10.12 (baseline)
  α=0.5:  98% refusal, PPL=13.94
  α=0.7:  98% refusal, PPL=90.87
  α=0.9:  12% refusal, PPL=908.89
  α=1.0:   0% refusal, PPL=1,656  ← model output is incoherent gibberish

HARMFUL DOMAIN — Subspace Ablation (k=8):
  α=0.0:  98% refusal, PPL=10.12 (baseline)
  α=0.8:  84% refusal, PPL=9.10   (still safe)
  α=0.9:  50% refusal, PPL=56.26
  α=1.0:   0% refusal, PPL=5,265  ← complete language model breakdown

Contrast with medical/legal at α=1.0:
  Medical subspace:  28% refusal, PPL=12.77  (acceptable)
  Legal subspace:    18% refusal, PPL=11.20  (acceptable)

Key insight: Harmful refusal is STRUCTURALLY PROTECTED by the model's
safety training. Removing it completely destroys coherence (PPL 1,600–5,200).
Medical/legal over-refusal can be surgically removed with minimal coherence
cost (PPL 11–13). This is the central argument for why subspace ablation is
a SAFE intervention for over-refusal — it only works on the domains where
the refusal is genuinely separable from core language capability.

      Result 7 as the synthesis/conclusion slide)

RESULT 9: Bootstrap Stability — Are Directions Reproducible?
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Method: 10× random 80% resampling of hidden states per domain.
Compute direction from each subsample, measure cosine similarity to
the original full-data direction.

Domain     Mean Sim   Std     Min     Interpretation
Harmful     0.913    0.166   0.443   High (2 outliers due to class imbalance*)
Medical     0.922    0.021   0.869   Very stable ✓
Legal       0.932    0.024   0.872   Very stable ✓

*Harmful outliers explained: only 2 "answered" samples in harmful dataset.
When one is dropped in 80% resample, mean(H_answered) shifts dramatically.
Excluding outliers: harmful clean mean = 0.997 (near-perfect stability).

Key insight: Refusal directions are STABLE across different prompt subsets —
not artifacts of specific prompts. Medical and legal have tight distributions
(std ≈ 0.02) well above the 0.90 stability threshold.

Plots: bootstrap_bars.png (RECOMMENDED for slides), bootstrap_strip.png, bootstrap_lines.png

RESULT 10: Activation Patching — Causal Head Identification
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Method: For each (layer, head) pair across layers 10–21 (12 layers × 32 heads):
  1. Corrupt run: ablate v_harmful at layer 16 (model answers instead of refusing)
  2. Clean run: same prompt with no ablation (model refuses)
  3. Patched run: corrupt run + patch this head's output from clean run
  4. AIE = P(refuse|patched) - P(refuse|corrupt)
     High AIE → this head is causally responsible for refusal

Results (N=5 prompts per head, layers 10–21):
  Non-zero AIE heads: 5 total, all in layers 19–21
  AIE resolution: 0.200 (= 1/5 prompts, minimum detectable effect)

Top causal heads:
  Layer 19, Head 0:  AIE=+0.200
  Layer 19, Head 1:  AIE=+0.200
  Layer 19, Head 7:  AIE=+0.200
  Layer 19, Head 21: AIE=+0.200
  Layer 21, Head 10: AIE=+0.200

Key finding: Causal signal concentrates in LATE layers (19–21), not early
or middle layers. Layers 10–18 show zero AIE — they carry information but
are not causally decisive for refusal. This is consistent with the
literature showing that safety-relevant computations happen in later
transformer layers.

Caveat: N=5 prompts per head gives coarse resolution (AIE granularity=0.2).
A full 20-prompt run would give finer resolution but would take ~5× longer.
This is marked as preliminary — qualitative pattern (late-layer causality)
is reliable; exact head ranking requires more prompts.

Plot: activation_patching_merged.png

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADDITIONAL 1: Injection Attack (Null Result — Supports Independence)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Method: Add v_d to hidden states of benign prompts: h' = h + α*v_d
Test: Does adding refusal direction force the model to refuse benign prompts?

Result: 0% refusal rate at ALL alpha values (0.0 to 3.0) for all domains.
The model continued answering "What is the capital of France?" normally
even at α=3.0.

Interpretation: Directions are DOMAIN-SPECIFIC — injecting a refusal vector
does not transfer refusal intent to unrelated contexts. This is consistent
with the independence finding (near-zero cosine similarities).
Note: Possible decode offset artifact may have contributed to null result.

Plot: injection_attack.png

ADDITIONAL 2: Zero-Shot Safety Probe (Weak Signal)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Method: Use score = h · v_harmful as a lightweight classifier.
No training required — classify as harmful if score > threshold τ.

Interpretation: v_harmful alone is NOT a reliable zero-shot classifier for

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADDITIONAL 4: Cross-Domain Transfer
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Method: Inject v_source into hidden states of target domain prompts.
Test whether refusal intent transfers across domains.

Transfers being tested:
  v_harmful → medical prompts   (does model treat medical as harmful?)
  v_harmful → legal prompts     (does model treat legal as harmful?)
  v_medical → harmful prompts   (does model treat harmful as medical?)
  v_legal   → harmful prompts   (does model treat harmful as legal?)

Transfer Effectiveness (TE) = max_refusal_rate(modified) - baseline_refusal_rate

Results:
  Transfer                  Baseline   Max    TE
  v_harmful → medical         45%      45%   +0.0%
  v_harmful → legal           75%      75%   +0.0%
  v_medical → harmful        100%     100%   +0.0%
  v_legal   → harmful        100%     100%   +0.0%

Transfer Effectiveness = 0% across ALL transfers at all alphas (0.0–2.0).

Key insight: Directions are completely DOMAIN-LOCKED. Injecting v_harmful into
medical prompts does not increase medical refusal at all, and vice versa.
This is the strongest possible confirmation of geometric independence —
not just near-zero cosine similarity, but zero functional transfer.
Supports the core independence claim from a causal (not just geometric) angle.

Plot: cross_domain_transfer.png

KEY NARRATIVE (10-min presentation order)
================================================================================
1. Problem: Standard benchmarks miss over-refusal on medical/legal domains
2. Method: Extract mean-difference direction vectors at layer 16 (4096-dim)
3. Finding 1: Vectors are geometrically independent (cosine sim ≈ 0)
4. Finding 2: Single vector ablation works for harmful (eff_rank ≈ 1) but
              CAUSES concept erasure for medical (eff_rank ≈ 31)
5. Explanation: SVD shows harmful is 1-dimensional, medical/legal are 26-31 dim
6. Fix: Subspace ablation (top-8 SVD vectors) reduces over-refusal without erasure
7. Tradeoff: Pareto frontier — α* ≈ 0.4-0.6 balances safety vs utility
8. Validation: Bootstrap confirms directions stable across prompt subsets
9. (Bonus) Activation patching: which specific attention heads cause refusal
10. Conclusion: Refusal mechanism is domain-decomposable; subspace ablation
                enables targeted, surgical reduction of over-refusal
